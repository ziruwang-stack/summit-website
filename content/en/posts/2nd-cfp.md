We invite submissions of RISC-V related presentations covering the following technical topics of interest:

## 1. Track on Artificial Intelligence

Scope: Focuses on the role of RISC-V across the full AI computing stack, from matrix/vector extension standardization, compilers and operator libraries, to AI frameworks and runtimes, as well as system integration for cloud clusters and edge-side heterogeneous deployment. Topics should address both standardization progress and real product or deployment cases.

## 2. Track on Datacenter / HPC

Scope: Focuses on application-class RISC-V adoption in datacenter and high-performance computing platforms, including server SoCs and platform specifications, firmware and boot flows (UEFI / ACPI / SBI), memory and interconnects including CXL, virtualization and cloud-native orchestration, confidential computing and trusted execution, HPC parallel frameworks, and scalability.

## 3. Track on Software Ecosystem

Scope: Covers the general software ecosystem from toolchains, kernels, firmware, distributions, and upper-layer runtimes to managed and language runtimes such as JVM, V8, .NET, Python, Rust, and Go, as well as JIT, debugging, performance analysis tools, multi-distribution support, and package ecosystems. The track highlights portable software enablement and ecosystem maturity.

## 4. Track on Design and Verification

Scope: Covers the full design and verification flow from microarchitecture and RTL design, agile development methodologies such as Chisel and SpinalHDL, to pre-silicon/formal verification, compliance testing, prototypes (FPGA / simulation), and tape-out. Topics include EDA tools, open source EDA, open source RISC-V cores, and verification methodologies for specification compliance.

## 5. Track on Automotive, Industrial & Robotics

Scope: Focuses on RISC-V adoption in safety-critical and real-time embedded domains, including automotive E/E architecture, industrial control, robotic motion and perception, low-altitude economy, and aerospace. Topics include functional safety (ISO 26262 / IEC 61508), real-time and deterministic systems, safety-critical certification, automotive-grade software stacks, and AUTOSAR adaptation.

## 6. Track on Embedded, IoT & Smart Hardware

Scope: Focuses on resource-constrained and cost-sensitive embedded and IoT mass-production scenarios, including MCU-class RISC-V, RTOS and bare-metal software, low power and power management, wireless connectivity, edge AI (TinyML), device security and trusted boot, mass production and supply chain, and the latest commercialization progress of consumer electronics products.

## 7. Track on Industry-Academia Collaboration, Innovation & Entrepreneurship

Scope: Covers RISC-V talent development and curriculum building, frontier research from universities and research institutions, collaboration with open source communities and standards organizations, startup and investment ecosystems, and frontier discussions around OPC.
